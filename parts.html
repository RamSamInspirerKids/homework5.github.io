Those 118 elements are grouped into 10 groups. Below there is more information on them.

Alkali Metal Group

Alkali Metal Group

Lithium, Sodium, Potassium, Rubidium, Caesium and Francium are in the Alkali Metal Group. This is because They are very reactive, electropositive, monovalent metals forming strongly alkaline hydroxides.

Alkaline Metal Group

Alkaline Metal Group

Beryllium, Mangnesium, Calcium, Strontium, Barium and Radium are in the Alkaline Metal Group. This is Because They are reactive, electropositive, divalent metals, and form basic oxides which react with water to form comparatively insoluble hydroxides.

Transition Metal Group

Transition Metal Group

There are 38 Elements in the Transition Metal Group. This is beacuse they have valence electrons in two shells instead of only one

Semimetals Group

Semimetals Group

Boron, Silicon, Germanium, Arsenic, Antimony, Tellurium and Polonium are in the Semimetals Group. This is becuase they have properties of Metals and Nonmetals. Also Semimetals are mostly used in technology.

Basic Metal Group

Basic Metal Group

Aluminum, Gallium, Indium, Tin, Thallium, Lead, Bismuth, Unutrium, Flerovium, Umunpentium and Livermorium are in the Basic Metal Group. This is because these element are common elements.

Halogen Group

Halogen Group

Flourine, Chlorine, Bromine, Iondine, Astatime, Tennessine are in the Halogen Group. This is Because when these elements react with metals they produce salts.

Nonmetals Group

Nonmetals Group

Hydrogen, Carbon, Nitrogen, Oxygen, Phosphours, Sulfur, Selenium are in the Nonmetals Group. This is beacause they are not a Metal.

Noble Gas Group

Noble Gas Group

Helium, Neon, Argon, Krypton, Xenon, Radon and Oganesson are in the Noble Gas Group. This is beacuse They are Rare gasses.

Lathanide Group

Actinide Group
